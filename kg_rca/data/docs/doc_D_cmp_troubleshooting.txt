CHEMICAL MECHANICAL PLANARIZATION (CMP) TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE

This guide lists failure modes observed in the CMP step, their causes, and the
equipment parameter involved in each cause.

Edge over-polish
Edge over-polish is a failure mode that occurs in the CMP step. It is caused by
excessive down force, which involves the down_force parameter.

Slurry maldistribution
Slurry maldistribution is a failure mode that occurs in the CMP step. It is
caused by low slurry flow, which involves the slurry_flow parameter.

Polish induced surface scratching
Polish induced surface scratching is a failure mode that occurs in the CMP step.
One cause of polish induced surface scratching is excessive down force, which
involves the down_force parameter. Another cause is slurry particle
agglomeration, which involves the slurry_flow parameter.
