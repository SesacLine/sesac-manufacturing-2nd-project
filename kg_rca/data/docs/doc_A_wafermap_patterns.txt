WAFER MAP DEFECT PATTERNS AND THEIR ASSOCIATED PROCESS STEPS

This note summarizes how the spatial signature of a wafer map defect points to
the process step most likely responsible. These associations are used as the
starting point for root cause analysis: the observed pattern narrows down which
process step to suspect first.

Edge-Ring pattern
An Edge-Ring pattern appears as a circular ring located along the wafer's edge.
This pattern is most commonly attributed to problems in the ETCH step. An
Edge-Ring pattern can also arise from thin-film nonuniformity created in the
DEPO step. When an Edge-Ring pattern is observed, the ETCH and DEPO steps should
be investigated first.

Center pattern
A Center pattern, also called a bulls-eye, is concentrated around the wafer
center. A Center pattern typically arises in the DEPO step, where deposition
nonuniformity produces a center-weighted signature. The Center pattern therefore
points to the DEPO step.

Scratch pattern
A Scratch pattern appears as an elongated line of failing dies across the wafer.
A Scratch pattern is frequently associated with the CMP step, where mechanical
abrasion during polishing gouges the wafer surface. A Scratch pattern can also
arise in the CLEAN step, where wafer handling and excessive megasonic energy
damage the surface. When a Scratch pattern is observed, the CMP and CLEAN steps
should be investigated first.
